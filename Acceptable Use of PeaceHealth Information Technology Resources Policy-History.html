PURPOSE

The purpose of this policy is to establish requirements for the acceptable use of PeaceHealth information technology resources to aid in protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PeaceHealth's information systems.

DEFINITIONS

  • Caregiver: PeaceHealth employee, volunteer, trainee, and other persons whose conduct, in the performance of work for PeaceHealth, is under the direct control of PeaceHealth, whether or not they are paid by PeaceHealth.

  • Confidential PeaceHealth Information: Information related to patients, caregivers, providers, financial data, and /or any other information pertaining to PeaceHealth business or proprietary information is to be considered strictly confidential unless specified otherwise.

  • Information Technology Resources (PeaceHealth electronic resources): Include but are not limited to desktop and laptop computers, software applications, wired and wireless networks, portable digital assistants (PDAs) and other hand held devices, telephones, pagers, cell phones, smart phones (including iPhones and Android phones and Windows phones), electronic tablets (including iPads and Android tablets and Windows tablets), portable workstations on wheels and carts, biomedical devices that collect patient information or provide life support or medical treatment, and any other device offering advanced (PC-like) capabilities that connect to the PeaceHealth computer network.

    COPY

  • Minimum Necessary: Limit to the amount reasonably necessary to achieve the purpose. The minimum necessary standard is a key protection of the HIPAA Privacy Rule.

  • Third Party: For the purpose of this policy, Third Party refers to any person or group performing work including but not limited to technical support, maintenance, implementation, or any other services performed on behalf of PeaceHealth (e.g., vendor, contracted client, sales and support, manufacturer, distributor, etc.).

  • User Credentials: User Credentials is a type of Identity Data. For PeaceHealth, user credentials consist of a User ID and password or badge access.

POLICY

It is the policy of PeaceHealth that the following requirements are implemented to support the use of information technology resources on PeaceHealth information systems that process, store, or transmit confidential information:

  1. Identification, Authentication, and Authorization

    1. PeaceHealth information system users are granted unique login credentials in accordance with the PeaceHealth Identification and Authentication policy and the Access to PeaceHealth Information Systems policy.

    2. Users do not obtain or use any other person's credentials (e.g., passwords, 3x3, access cards, or other unique IDs). Users do not disclose their own credentials or allow their credentials to be used by any other person.

    3. The ability to access PeaceHealth information technology resources does not imply authorization to access. Users are responsible for applying the "minimum necessary" standard when accessing PeaceHealth information technology resources.

  2. General User Responsibilities and Limitations

    1. All users are responsible for accessing PeaceHealth information technology resources in a manner that supports information system security and the privacy and security of confidential PeaceHealth information.

    2. Users obtain adequate training in the acceptable use of PeaceHealth information technology resources. Users do not attempt to self-train or practice their skills by accessing confidential information beyond what is minimally necessary to do their jobs in accordance with the Confidentiality, Security, and Privacy of PeaceHealth Information policy.

    3. Users do not allow access (directly or remotely) to PeaceHealth systems to make unauthorized changes.

    4. Users do not accept, download, or install unauthorized software.

    5. Users understand that local storage is not backed up and, therefore, data stored locally (typically on the C: drive) may be lost due to hardware failure, system upgrade, or other eventuality.

      COPY

    6. PeaceHealth reserves the right to discontinue use of any services if that service is deemed to violate existing policies or if a change in law impacts such a change.

    7. All users are responsible for enrolling in a PeaceHealth-supported Multi-Factor Authentication method which is required for any accounts accessing PeaceHealth systems.

  3. Lost Devices and Security Incidents

    1. Users report suspected information privacy and security incidents, such as potential privacy breaches and lost or stolen devices, to their supervisor and to the Service Desk, in accordance with the Reporting Information Privacy and Security Incidents procedure.

  4. Storage, Transmission, and Disposal of Confidential Information

    1. Storage of confidential information must be done in an approved, secure manner.

    2. Users may not store confidential information on local storage (typically the C: drive) without authorization.

    3. Electronic transmission of confidential information must be done in a secure manner in accordance with the System and Communication Protection policy and the Transportation and Electronic Transmission of Confidential Information policy.

    4. The capture of photographs, video recordings, and audio recordings that include confidential information inside a PeaceHealth facility is permitted only as defined within the Photography, Videography, and Audio Recording policy.

    5. The confidential information stored in temporary locations, such as removable media or on local drives, must be completely deleted, removed, and/or destroyed as soon as possible after use, and done so in accordance with TSP procedure.

    6. Disposal of confidential information that is stored on electronic resources is done in

      accordance with the Disposal of Confidential Information policy.

  5. Desktop and Portable Devices

    1. Users logged into PeaceHealth information technology resources secure the computer when leaving the immediate vicinity by locking the device or by logging out in accordance with the Work Area Information Security policy.

    2. Some computing devices and applications automatically log the user out of the application or secure the device after a specified period of inactivity. Users are prohibited from modifying or disabling the auto-logoff or auto screen lock features.

    3. Encryption is required on all PeaceHealth-owned desktops, laptops, portable devices, and removable media, with limited, authorized exceptions.

  6. Security Requirements for Handling Portable Devices

    COPY

    1. The following standards pertain to portable devices that are owned by PeaceHealth or connect to PeaceHealth resources such as email:

      1. Keep your portable device with you at all times unless not practicably possible. If you need to leave your device in your vehicle, store it in the trunk before you get to your destination. If you do not have a trunk, make sure the portable device is stored out- of-sight and always lock your vehicle. Never leave a portable device in plain sight when leaving it in your vehicle.

      2. Do not store your portable device in your vehicle overnight, even if your vehicle is parked at your home.

      3. Always carry your portable device on a plane; do not check it in your luggage.

      4. Do not leave your portable device unattended in a public place.

      5. Store portable devices in secured areas when not in use.

  7. Use of Internet and Other External Resources

    1. Access to internet resources (e.g., internet browsing and streaming media) must comply with the Use of PeaceHealth Internet Services policy.

    2. Access to social media must comply with the PeaceHealth Social Media Policy.

    3. Use of PeaceHealth email must comply with the Use of PeaceHealth Email policy.

    4. Users do not use PeaceHealth credentials (username and password) to register or sign on to external sites including those used for PeaceHealth business (e.g., Insurance portals, professional memberships, state and other quality registries etc.). PeaceHealth's implementation of Microsoft Office 365 is not considered to be an external site.

    5. Access to internet sites, portals (e.g., Payor portals, government or state or other external sites) or other cloud services (e.g., sharefile.com) that are used to support PeaceHealth business workflows shall only be used in accordance with established agreements (e.g.,

      agency, business associate agreement, etc.).

    6. Users are prohibited from using their personal email accounts (e.g., Gmail, Hotmail) to send or receive PeaceHealth confidential information.

    7. Access to the internet using PeaceHealth-owned devices from locations outside of PeaceHealth facilities, such as coffee shops, airports, hotels, your home, etc., must be performed only through TSP approved methods.

  8. Personal Devices and Connectivity to PeaceHealth Network

    1. Devices not owned by PeaceHealth may not be directly connected to the PeaceHealth internal network or to PeaceHealth-owned devices without authorization. Examples of prohibited activities include, but are not limited to, the following:

      1. Connecting personal laptop via network cable;

      2. Connecting personal flash drive via USB;

        COPY

      3. Charging personal smartphone via USB; and

      4. Syncing personal device via Bluetooth.

    2. Guest internet access is available via the PH-Guest-WiFi wireless network for all Caregivers, patients, and visitors. Users will be asked to agree to PeaceHealth terms and services annually to remain connected to the PH-Guest-WiFi wireless network.

    3. Users should not connect to the PH-Guest-WiFi wireless network with PeaceHealth- owned devices unless the configuration is set-up by TSP.

    4. Remote access to the PeaceHealth network and/or to certain applications may be available to authorized users. Please see the TSP Crossroads page for more information on approved access methods.

    5. PeaceHealth reserves the right to deny, restrict, or revoke access at any time.

    6. PeaceHealth is not responsible for damage, harm, complications with the device, inability to access, failures, loss of data, or loss of functioning associated with use of PeaceHealth resources with a non-PeaceHealth-owned device.

    7. All PeaceHealth information stored on personally owned devices is the property of PeaceHealth and must be destroyed or returned to PeaceHealth upon termination of employment or association with PeaceHealth.

    8. Caregivers who need assistance in the use of approved access methods may contact the PeaceHealth Service Desk [x6464 or 1-800-452-1425].

  9. Third Party Relationships

    1. Users may not troubleshoot third-party applications with external vendors or outside agencies nor engage with third parties to download software for troubleshooting purposes. All requests for troubleshooting must be processed via the Service Desk.

    2. Third-party vendor devices are strictly prohibited from connecting to PeaceHealth's network

      without approval.

    3. Third-party supported devices must adhere to all security controls established by PeaceHealth as well as be compliant with established Service Level Agreements (SLA), Business Associate Agreements (BAA), or other contractual arrangements.

      1. Third parties will not be allowed administrative privileges without the express approval from TSP leadership or their designees.

    4. PeaceHealth prohibits the donation of technology resources to third parties.

  10. Prohibited Use

    The following activities are strictly prohibited:

    1. Binding PeaceHealth or representing PeaceHealth to any contractual agreement or obligation over the internet for any type of service without authorization.

    2. Engaging in any activity while utilizing PeaceHealth information technology resources that is illegal under local, state, or federal law, or that violates PeaceHealth policies;

      COPY

    3. Harming, threatening, or attempting to harm the reputation of any workforce member or PeaceHealth, including using inflammatory or threatening language, racial slurs, etc.;

    4. Sending, knowingly receiving, uploading, downloading, transmitting, or accessing material inappropriate in a work environment, such as pornography;

    5. Accessing, implementing, or deploying computer or network security utilities, such as port scanners, vulnerability scanners, network monitoring tools, stress testers, or password cracking tools, without authorization;

    6. Causing harm to PeaceHealth information technology resources, including the introduction of computer viruses, spyware, or any other malware; and

    7. Sending, knowingly receiving, uploading, downloading, transmitting, or using any material not consistent with PeaceHealth Mission, Vision, and Values;

  11. Personal Use of PeaceHealth Information Technology Resources

    1. Users must not jeopardize the tax-exempt status of PeaceHealth by using PeaceHealth electronic resources for personal business, political purposes, or other purposes that result in personal, financial, or other gain.

    2. Incidental use for personal purposes is permitted when it is compliant with other PeaceHealth policies and procedures and it does not interfere with work performance or the operations of the department.

    3. Personal document storage on local workstation and network drives (c:\local device, m:\personal, n:\department shared, etc.) is prohibited unless authorization is provided based on business need. TSP reserves the right to remove any content where they deem appropriate to support business operations.

  12. Monitoring and Oversight

    1. Users understand that PeaceHealth owns and retains the right to monitor the use of PeaceHealth electronic resources and may access information stored on PeaceHealth devices or transmitted over the network in the following circumstances:

      1. As necessary for PeaceHealth work and business-related reasons such as a person who is on vacation or sick leave and access to some files is needed to continue PeaceHealth business;

      2. When it reasonably appears necessary to do so to protect the integrity, confidentiality, availability, or functioning of PeaceHealth electronic resources, or to protect PeaceHealth from liability;

      3. There is reasonable cause to believe that the user has violated, or is violating, PeaceHealth policy or is engaging in unlawful activity;

      4. An account appears to be engaged in unusual or unusually excessive activity, as indicated by the monitoring of general activity and usage patterns; or

        COPY

      5. It is otherwise required by law.

    2. Subject to any applicable laws, users understand that PeaceHealth may disclose the results of monitoring, including the contents and records of individual communications, to appropriate personnel, including regulatory or law enforcement agencies, and may use those results in disciplinary and/or legal proceedings.

  13. Management Responsibilities

    1. Managers and supervisors of users, whether they are employed caregivers, non- PeaceHealth- employed, temporary, or contractors, are responsible for meeting the following requirements for users under their direction:

    2. Users have received adequate training in proper use of PeaceHealth electronic resources prior to access;

    3. User accounts for access are set up, maintained, and terminated appropriately;

    4. The TSP representative for the department, facility, or EHI Client site is immediately notified when a user's roles and responsibilities have changed, such as when the user transfers to a different position or department, departs on extended leave of absence, has terminated their employment or their contractual relationship has ended, etc.;

    5. All requirements to reinstate a user's access to PeaceHealth electronic resources are met prior to the user's return to work after a significant absence; and

    6. User's assigned PeaceHealth electronic resources are collected and returned when use of those resources are no longer necessary.

COMPLIANCE

Individuals included in the scope of this policy are required to comply with the policy and all associated policies, standards, and procedures. Violations are subject to removal of access and/ or corrective

action, up to and including termination of employment or other business relationship with PeaceHealth. Any deviations from security standards observed in the production environment must be communicated to the Information Security department immediately upon discovery. If an exception to this policy is necessary, it must be submitted in accordance with the Information Security Policy and Standards Exceptions policy.

HELP

Further information may be obtained by contacting the PeaceHealth Service Desk.
